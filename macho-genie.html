01

Overview

Macho Genie became an opportunity to translate a distinct brand point of view into a practical design system: one that could support product needs without sanding down the personality of the interface.

02

The challenge

The system needed to be legible for product teams and flexible for growth, while still maintaining a tone that felt expressive, characterful, and specific to the brand.

03

Building the foundation

The first step was to establish a shared foundation: naming logic, spacing conventions, type hierarchy, and the structural rules that could anchor every future screen and component.

04

Tokens and visual rules

Tokens were defined not only as engineering handoff units, but as visual rules: a set of decisions around type, color, stroke, radius, and spacing that could keep the system recognizable at every scale.

05

Core components

Core UI primitives were organized to cover repeated patterns first, making the system genuinely usable for product work rather than simply decorative documentation.

06

Interaction states

Hover, focus, loading, empty, and error states were treated as part of the core language so the product could feel complete and consistent even outside polished hero moments.

07

Applying the system

Once the pieces were stable, the value of the system became visible in application: faster screen building, clearer decision-making, and a more coherent product experience across flows.

08

Outcome

The case study shows a system designed to support both expression and scale: a practical toolkit with enough visual intent to feel like a real product voice rather than a neutral framework.
