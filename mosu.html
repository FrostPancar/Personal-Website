01

Overview

MOSU was a material-driven design venture built from concept to object to storefront. The project combined material research, additive manufacturing, object design, and a consumer-facing storefront into one connected system.

Scope
Product Design, Brand Direction, Ecommerce, Rendering
Role
Founder and Design Lead
02

Challenge

The challenge was to create a business where environmental thinking and aesthetic appeal reinforced each other. The products, visual language, and storefront all needed to feel coherent while still working within the practical constraints of 3D printing and a small founder-led launch.

03

Product system

I treated the objects as both functional products and sculptural artifacts. Form, texture, and silhouette were shaped by fabrication logic, material behavior, and the physical character of additive manufacturing rather than being designed first and rationalized later.

04

Brand and commerce

The digital experience was designed to extend the product story rather than simply display inventory. The brand language, renders, product framing, and interface structure worked together to position MOSU as a thoughtful design venture rather than a hobbyist storefront.

05

Storytelling assets

MOSU needed to communicate not only what the products were, but why they existed. That meant building a visual system around renders, product imagery, and editorial framing that could give the venture a clear point of view and make the sustainability story feel tangible rather than abstract.

06

Outcome

MOSU became both a business experiment and a multidisciplinary design exercise. It showed how sustainable thinking could shape the object, the narrative, and the customer experience at the same time, across a full system rather than a single deliverable.
