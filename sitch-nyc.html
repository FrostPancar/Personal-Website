01

Project overview

Sitch is an AI dating app that positions itself less like a swipe platform and more like a personalized matchmaker. I reworked the brand and website to make that idea feel more culturally distinct, giving it a visual world that blended vintage old-money cues with a sharper urban-chic tone.

Scope
Website Redesign, Branding, Style Guide, Social Kit
Role
Creative Direction and Design
02

Challenge

The challenge was to move Sitch away from looking like a generic dating product and toward a more specific emotional register. The brand needed to feel aspirational, intimate, and style-conscious while still explaining an AI-powered product clearly enough to convert new users.

03

Creative direction

The new direction pulled from two overlapping references: vintage old-money elegance and contemporary urban polish. That meant using more editorial pacing, more elevated typography, and a more selective visual hierarchy so the brand could feel aspirational without becoming cold or over-styled.

04

Web design

The website redesign translated that new brand position into a clearer landing experience. The goal was to make the app’s promise legible quickly while giving the site enough atmosphere and taste to feel like a real brand world rather than a functional acquisition page.

05

Style guide and social kit

To make the new direction usable beyond the website, I built a style guide and social toolkit covering typography, image treatment, layout rules, crops, and repeatable social formats. The aim was to give Sitch a system that could stay recognizable across fast-moving marketing outputs.

06

Rollout across channels

Once the visual system was defined, it could extend across the website, social posts, launch assets, and broader campaign touchpoints without needing to be reinvented each time. That made the brand feel more unified and more intentional wherever it showed up.

07

Outcome

The result was a more distinctive digital presence for Sitch: a website with a stronger point of view, a sharper brand world, and a toolkit that could support ongoing social and marketing output with more consistency and taste.
